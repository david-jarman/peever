NEWS ITEM FROM THE WILMOT REVIEW...JAN. 9, 1914


Entire block of business houses wiped out by early morning blaze

One of the worst fires in the history of the county occured at Peever last Friday morning when an entire block on the west side of Main street was wiped out by a fire starting from an unknown origin in the rear of the Sjoberg harness shop. The loss is conservatively estimated at $50,000.00, covered by about $25,000.00 insurance. No one was injured.
The fire was first discovered about 4:30 in the morning, and soon a bucket brigade was formed from the town well. A strong northwest wind was blowing and but little could be accomplished by the fire fighters while the flames were devouring the frame buildings, seven in number, composing the block. (Block 3).
When the fire reached the cross street the fighters got in some good work and checked the progress of the flames toward the buildings across the street to the east and to the next block on the south. The buildings in the adjoining block ignited several times, but persistant efforts on the part of the fire fighters soon extinguished the fires and saved the balance of the town. The buildings destroyed were the Fred Wilson Bowling Alley, W. H. Hines hardware store, Central Hotel, John Noble Law Office, Sjoberg Harness Shop, the Woodman Hall and a vacant building. Two residences, one belonging  to R.A. Aney, liveryman, and the other to Mrs. Opitz, were also destroyed.
The loss fell heaviest on W.H. Hines, a brother of Dr. Hines, V.S. of this city, who had upward of $10,000.00 tied up in buildings and stock. His insurance policy lapsed the first of the year and had not been renewed.
The fire was under control by 8:30 in the morning. The Milwaukee Road rushed a tank of water up from Milbank but it arrived too late to be of any use. The firemen were compelled to depend on the small flow from the town wells.
