THESE ARE COURT HOUSE RECORDS OF THE ACTUAL SALE OF THE LAND THAT WAS TO BECOME THE PEEVER TOWN SITE. (Note that the day George Brunell received his patent he sold it immediately to Spackman, Peever and Miller.)


12/6/1900…Patent from USA to George Brunell..Nw1/4 of NW ¼ of Sec. 8..124…50
12/6/1900…George Brunell to HL Spackman, TH Peever, NH Miller…NW ¼ of NW1/4 of 8-124-50
12/14/1900………Platted the town of Peever…and signed by the above three men….Spackman, Peever and Miller.

A live and growing town in Roberts County, on the Chicago, Milwaukee and St. Paul RR, 10 miles southeast of Sisseton. It is surrounded by a rich farming country, with the usual town improvements. Population 300.
